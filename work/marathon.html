Marathon

A speculative redesign of Marathon's in-game interface — exploring how Bungie's sci-fi extraction shooter could feel sharper, faster, and more legible at the moments that matter most: loadout, combat, and recovery.

Responsibilities

Owned the redesign end-to-end — research into existing screens, system mapping, visual direction, component design, and motion explorations. Documented decisions in a public case study and YouTube breakdown.

Per-screen breakdowns are documented below.

Screens

The HUD redesign focused on hierarchy: critical info reads in a glance, secondary state stays available without crowding the play space.

I explored typographic systems that hold up at speed, status icons drawn from a single geometric grid, and a color logic where blue carries identity, white carries data, and warning states earn their visual weight.
